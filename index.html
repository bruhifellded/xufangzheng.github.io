    What is Chinese opera


    Chinese opera is also called xiqu which is form of musical theatre which is passed down from the early days of China, and gradually evolved and matured at the song dynasty.

    Six dynasties to Tang:


    The most early form of the Chinese drama is the Canjun Opera which inspired a popular performance xiangsheng(cross talking) both involving two performers where one act as a more serious character(adjutant) and is ridiculed by a jester/funny character(Grey Hawk) by the end of tang dynasty it have become a more complexed plot and dramatic twists and it involved at least four performers

    Song to Qing:


    By the Song Dynasty, Canjun Opera had become a performance that involved singing and dancing, and led to the development of Zaju and Nanxi Also different roles got introduced to the opera

    1912–1949:


    The introduction of Western plays in China influenced Chinese playwrights to imitate that form. Cao Yu, for example, created plays such as Thunderstorm, Sunrise, Wilderness, and Peking Man.

    1949–1985:


    During the development of the Republic of China, Peking opera experienced a rise in popularity as a traditional art form. After the Chinese Communist Revolution, a new genre called Schinggo opera emerged, incorporating revolutionary energy from folk traditions and being influenced by European music. However, many opera troupes were disbanded and banned, except for eight "model operas." In 1976, Beijing Opera experienced a revival and continued to be a popular form of entertainment on stage and television.

    Costume and make-up


    Face painting plays a very significant role in Chinese opera as these face painting combines colors and patterns to represent there characteristic

    • Red - bravery, fidelity, loyalty, chastity
    • Black - impatience, straightforwardness, vulgarity, rudeness
    • White - cunning, treachery, villainy, betrayal
    • Blue - ferocity, courage, uncontrollability, cruelty, violence
    • Yellow - cleverness, secretiveness, mystery, evil
    • Purple - loyalty, filial piety, age
    • Green - bravery, purpose, an evil spirit
    • Gold and silver - godhood, divinity, the supernatural (monsters, spirits, demons)
